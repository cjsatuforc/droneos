The software uses Google map API to plot the path. The path co-ordinates are then saved in database. On the user command, the software fetches the points of the path that is set active. The software then communicates with the gps and gives signals i.e. pulse width modulation (PWM) to quad copter using Raspberry pi GPIO pins, to guide it to the destination. At the destination point, it takes pictures for the time interval specified by the user and then comes back to its home place. It is a complete auto flight system with no interference of human commands. It uses a camera, raspberry pi, gps receiver and a quad copter.

4.1 QUAD COPTER

The RC quad copter comes into the category of multi rotor RC which is the latest and fastest growing expansion in radio controlled vertical lift platforms that are able to take off vertically, hover, and fly in all directions. There are 4 propellers arranged in a quad copter, in a cross type configuration. They are completely aerodynamically unstable and undeniably require computer control for steady flight.

The Quad Copter is the simplest type of multicopter, having opposite pair of motors spinning in opposite directions. This permits the copter to turn (Yaw) right or left by just speeding up one pair and slowing the other pair of motors. Horizontal motion is able by speeding up motors (increasing thrust) on one side and reducing it on the other. This would make the copter to tilt Roll or Pitch in the chosen direction of motion and thrust is re-equalized. The angle of the copter is usually representing its speed in that direction. To fly, the copter compensates for disturbance ‘gusts of wind’ by tilting automatically against the direction of the disturbance. In order to accomplish this, the copter has electronic gyros which sense level in 3 dimensions. They have electronic accelerometers which sense displacement in 3 dimensions. Altitude control or change is procured by accelerating or decelerating all motors simultaneously.

4.1.1 Advantages of Using Quad Copter

When comparison comes up between quad copters and scaled helicopters, there are several advantages in using quad copters. Firstly, quad copters do not require mechanical linkages to vary the rotor blade pitch angle when they spin. This makes the design very simple and keeps the vehicle maintained.

Secondly, the utilization of four rotors support each individual rotor to have a smaller diameter than the equivalent helicopter rotor, allows them to possess less kinetic energy during flight. This reduces the damage caused when the rotors hit anything. For small-scale UAVs, this makes the vehicles safer for close interaction. Some small-scale quad copters possess frames that enclose the rotors, permitting flights by more challenging surroundings and lower the risk of damaging the vehicles.

One of the most fascinating components about quad copters is their ability to self stabilize in air. This autonomous leveling that takes places makes flying them very 'beginner' cordial by abstracting that even a small need for control out of the pilot’s hands. In the impeccable scenario a quad copter should stay completely level in the air and hold its same position above the ground. The pilot can then move it by altering its altitude, spinning it, or moving it in its lateral directions.

4.1.2 Significant Design Considerations for Copters

4.1.3 WORKING OF A QUAD COPTER

A quad copter has four rotors all collaborate to engender upward thrust and each rotor hoists only 1/4 of the weight, so we can utilize less potent and therefore more frugal motors. The quad copter’s movement is controlled by varying the relative thrusts of each rotor.

These rotors are aligned in a square, two on opposite sides of the square rotate in right-handed direction and the other two rotate in the opposite direction. If all rotors turn in the same direction, the craft would turn just like the customary helicopter without tail rotor. Yaw is brought by unbalanced aerodynamic torques. The aerodynamic torque of the first rotors pair cancelled out with the torque caused by the second pair which rotates in the exact opposite direction, so if all four rotors apply equal thrust the quad copter will stay in the same direction.

To maintain balance the quad copter must be constantly taking quantifications from the sensors, and making adjustments to the speed of each rotor to keep the body level. Usually these adjustments are done autonomously by a refined control system on the quad copter in order to stay faultlessly balanced. A quad copter has four controllable degrees of liberation: Yaw, Roll, Pitch, and Altitude. Each degree of liberation can be controlled by adjusting the thrusts of each rotor.

4.1.3.1 Yaw

Yaw (turning left and right) is controlling of turning up the speed of the regular rotating motors and taking away power from the counter rotating; by taking away the same amount that you put in with the regular rotors produces no extra lift (it won’t go higher) but since the counter torque is now less, the quad rotor rotates as control becomes a matter of which motor gets more power and which one gets less.

4.1.3.2 Roll

Roll (tilting left and right) is controlling of increasing speed on one motor and lowering on the opposite one.

4.1.3.3 Pitch

Pitch (moving up and down, similar to nodding) is controlling the same way as roll, but using the second set of motors. This may be kind of confusing, but roll and pitch are determined from where the “front” of the thing is, and in a quad rotor they are basically interchangeable.

One must take a note that it’s important to decide that which way is front and be consistent on that or otherwise device may go out of control.

For example, to roll or pitch, one rotor’s thrust is decreased and the opposite rotor’s thrust is increased by the same amount. It would help quad copter to tilt. The quad copter when happens to tilt, the force vector is split into a horizontal component and a vertical component. This causes two things to happen: First, the quad copter will begin to travel opposite the direction of the newly created horizontal component. Second, because the force vector has been split, the vertical component will be smaller, causing the quad copter to begin to fall. In order to keep the quad copter from falling, the thrust of each rotor is increased to compensate.

4.1.4.2 Motors

Brush DC Motors versus Brushless Motors

Brush DC Motors

Dc brush motors are one of the simplest types of motors. Sans the dc supply or battery required for operation, a typical brush dc motor consists of an armature (a.k.a., rotor), a commutator, brushes, an axle, and a field magnet. The properties of motor are determined by the material it’s made of, the number of coils wound around it, and the density of the coils. The armature or rotor is an electromagnet, and the field magnet is a permanent magnet. The commutator is a split-ring device covered around the axel that physically contacts the brushes, which are connected to opposite poles of the power source.

The brushes charge the commutator inversely in polarity to the permanent magnet, in turn causing the armature to rotate. The rotation’s direction, clockwise and or counterclockwise, can be reversed easily by reversing the polarity of the brushes, i.e., reversing the leads on the battery.

Disadvantages of Brush Motors

  • Less effective

  • Electrically noisy: The switching action of the commutators continuously creating and breaking inductive circuits produces a great deal of electrical and electromagnetic noise.

  • Lifespan:  As they are in perpetual physical contact with the shaft, brushes and commutators wear out.



Brushless DC Motors

BLDC motors lack brushes, But their design differences are bit more refined (BLDC) Motor .A BLDC motor mounts its permanent magnets, usually four or more, around the perimeter of the rotor in a cross design. Efficiency is a primary selling feature for BLDC motors. The rotor is the sole bearer of the magnets that is why it requires no power, i.e., no connections, no commutator, and no brushes. On the other hand, the motor employs control circuitry. To detect where the rotor is at certain times, BLDC motors employ, along with controllers, rotary encoders or a Hall sensor. BLDC motors are synchronous motors. It tells that their rotors and stators turn at the same frequency. They come in single-, dual-, and tri-phase shapes.

When looking for the brushless, notice about the specifications, especially the “Kv-rating“.The Kv-rating indicates how many RPMs (Revolutions per minute) the motor will do, only if with x-number of volts. The RPMs can be calculated in this method: RPM=Kv*U.



Advantages of Brushless DC Motors

Brushless motors spin in much higher speed and use less power at the same speed than DC motors.

  • Brushless motors don’t lose power in the brush-transition like the DC motors do, so it’s more energy competent.

  • Long lifespan: No brushes to wear out.

  • Low maintenance: No brushes to replace.

4.1.4.3 Propellers

On each of the brushless motors there are mounted a propeller, but the 4 propellers are genuinely not alike. The front and the back propellers are tilted to the right, while the left and right propellers are tilted to the left.

To avoid body spinning, two rotors rotate in the opposite directions to the other two. By making the propeller pairs spin in each direction, but also having opposite tilting, all of them will provide lifting thrust without spinning in the same direction. This makes it possible for the Quad Copter to stabilize the yaw rotation, which is the rotation around itself.

The propellers come in different diameters and pitches (tilting). To select which one to use depends upon frame size, and when that decision is made select motors according to that.  Some of the standard propeller sizes used for Quad Copters are:

EPP1045  10 diameter and 4.5 pitch (this is the most popular one, good for mid-sized quads.)

EPP0938   9 diameter and 3.8 pitch (used in smaller quads.)

Rules for Selecting Propellers

For selecting the propellers always follow these rules:

  • The larger the diameter and the pitch, the more thrust the propeller can produce. It also requires more power to drive it, but it will be able to lift more weight.

  • When decide to choose high RPM (Revolutions per minute) motors you should go for the smaller or mid-sized propellers. Whenever use the low RPM motors you should go for the larger propellers as you can run into difficulties with the small ones not being able to lift the quad at low speed.

4.1.4.4 ESC – Electronic Speed Controller

The direct supply of DC power will not turn the motors on because the brushless motors are multi-phased, normally 3 phases. That’s where the Electronic Speed Controllers (ESC) comes into play. The ESC generates three high frequency signals with different but controllable phases frequently to keep the motor turning. The ESC is also able to source a lot of current as the motors can pull a lot of power.

The ESC is an inexpensive motor controller board. It is having a battery input and a three phase output for the motor. Each ESC is controlled autonomously by a PPM signal (similar to PWM). The frequency of the signals also changing a lot, but for a Quad copter it is suggested the controller should maintain high enough frequency signal, so the motor speeds can be accustomed quick enough for optimal stability (i.e. at least 200 Hz or even better 300 Hz PPM signal). ESC can also be controlled through I2C but these controllers are much more expensive.

While making a selection for a suitable ESC, the most important factor to be considered is the source current. Always choose an ESC with at least 10 A or more in sourcing current as what the motor (that is in use) will require. Second most important factor here is the programming facilities. It means in some ESC, it is allowed to use different signals frequency range other than only between 1 ms to 2 ms range, but can be adjusted to whatever need. This is specifically useful for custom controller board.

  • How much influence the battery weight should have on the total weight?

A good rule of thumb is that you with four EPP1045 propellers and four Kv=1000 rated motor will get the number of minutes of full throttle flight time as the same number of amp-hours in the device’s battery capacity. This means that if your device has a 4000mAh battery, you will get around 4 minutes of full throttle flight time though with a 1KG total heft you will get around 16 minutes of hover.

Battery Discharge Rate

Additional important factor is the discharge rate which is stated by the C-value. The C-value together with the battery capacity specifies how much current can be drawn from the battery. Maximum current that can be track down can be calculated as:

MaxCurrent = DischargeRate x Capacity

For example if there is a battery that has a discharge rate of 30C and a capacity of 2000 mAh. This battery will be able to make a source maximum of 30Cx2000mAh = 60A. So in this type of state make sure that the total amount of current drawn by motors won’t go beyond 60A.

def directions_to(self, P2):

"""

Tells bearing (angle) and distance to P2

"""

5.4.1 DISTANCE CALCULATION

lat1 = self.lat

lon1 = self.lng

lat2 = P2.lat

lon2 = P2.lng

#Haversine Formuala to find vertical angle and distance

lon1, lat1, lon2, lat2 = map(float, [lon1, lat1, lon2, lat2])

lon1, lat1, lon2, lat2 = map(radians, [lon1, lat1, lon2, lat2])

dlon = lon2 - lon1

dlat = lat2 - lat1

a = sin(dlat/2)**2 + cos(lat1) * cos(lat2) * sin(dlon/2)**2

c = 2 * atan2(sqrt(a), sqrt(1-a))

base = 6371 * c

5.4.2 CALCULATING HORIZONTAL BEARING

def calc_bearing(lat1, lon1, lat2, lon2):

dLon = lon2 - lon1

y = sin(dLon) * cos(lat2)

x = cos(lat1) * sin(lat2) \

- sin(lat1) * cos(lat2) * cos(dLon)

return atan2(y, x)

bearing = calc_bearing(lat1, lon1, lat2, lon2)

bearing = degrees(bearing)

return dict(distance=base, direction=bearing)

In our project Drone Operating System, we have performed the white box testing against the following inputs and outputs:

  • When the virtual environment is activated, whether it shows the map or not.

  • When the click is made on the map, whether it drops a point there or not.

  • When the next click is made, whether it joins the first point to the current one through a line or not.

  • Whether the data is saved in the database or not

  • When the user enters the name, whether the database updates itself with the newly entered path or not.

  • Whether the data is fetch able from the database for calculation or not

  • When the GPS is activated, whether is displays data or not

  • When the GPS is working, whether it updates its values with the time intervals or not

  • Whether the code is executable in a memory card or not

  • Whether the code in Raspberry pi is working or not

  • Whether the quad copter moves on the plotted track or not

  • Whether the quad copter changes its direction or not

  • Whether the copter returns back to its original position or not

LIMITATIONS OF (DRONE- OS):


  • Simple toy helicopters cause weight issue; it cannot pick the weight of raspberry pi, gps receiver and battery. Helicopter that operates on petrol can pick weight but is very expensive. Moreover, toy helicopters are difficult to operate by the new users even if controlled by remotes. Due to the weight issue quad copter that picks weight of 1000g is a better choice. It was too expensive and not easily available but less expensive than helicopters operated on petrol.

  • In order to run RPi need light weight lithium batteries that can give 5V power supply to RPi. The lithium batteries available are of 3.5V.So, the two batteries must be connected in parallel to provide maximum of 6V to run RPi.
